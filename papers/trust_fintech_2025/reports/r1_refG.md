## General Referee Report
**Round:** 1
**Overall verdict:** Minor revision

### Contribution assessment
The extension takes Yang (2025)'s continuous-intensity (Wells-Fargo-exposure × post) county DiD and (i) reproduces the published Table 5 triple difference deterministically as a blocking gate, then (ii) re-estimates the average-county double difference with a cross-fitted DoubleML PLR on the two-way-FE-residualized panel across four learners, plus a GATE/CATE by the paper's own NonRep dimension. The value-add is a genuine functional-form-flexibility check on the inherited identification (the FE-adjusted effect survives ML-partialling of the exogenous county covariates: 0.019 TWFE → 0.014–0.022 DML, all significant) together with disciplined bad-control handling. Pleased-test: yes — this is a clean, well-scoped worked example of the replication gate with honest, traceable artifacts; I would be pleased to have produced it, subject to one wording fix.

### Essential issues (must be fixed before the result can be trusted)
1. **The GATE "groups are statistically distinguishable" claim overstates the evidence.** `hte_results.json:gate_NonRep` gives tercile effects T1 = +0.0202, T2 = +0.0108, T3 = +0.0269 with joint CIs [−0.0043, 0.0448], [−0.0108, 0.0325], [0.0100, 0.0438] and `joint_ci_excludes_zero = [false, false, true]`. The library's `distinguishable` flag is `any(joint_ci_excludes_zero)` (`_gate_record`, recast_dml.py) — i.e. it fires when *one* group is jointly non-zero, NOT when the groups differ from *each other*. Here only the top NonRep tercile is jointly significant; the three tercile effects are non-monotonic and their joint CIs overlap heavily, so they are not statistically distinguishable from one another. The report prose therefore over-claims the very dimension that is the DML analogue of the paper's central triple-difference mechanism, conflicting with the small-n honesty rule (skill Step 3.6) and with the block's own EXPLORATORY caveat. *Action:* reword to state what the JSON supports: "the exposure × post effect is jointly significant only in the highest-NonRep tercile (T3); the three tercile point estimates (0.020 / 0.011 / 0.027) are non-monotonic and their joint CIs overlap, so the terciles are not statistically distinguishable from one another — consistent with the paper's direction but underpowered at this n." Optionally relabel the JSON `distinguishable` semantics (or rename to `any_group_significant`) so the field name stops implying between-group separation.

### Suggestions (non-binding)
1. **Surface the n-gap magnitude in the headline more explicitly.** The replicated triple-diff n = 8174 vs published 7335 is an ~11% gap (well outside the §2.2 1% PARTIAL band); the build correctly handles this by passing `n_published=None` so n is "documented, not gated" (replication_check.json `tier_basis`), and the text discloses it. This is defensible, but the summary-lead ("within ~2%") foregrounds only the coefficient deviation; one clause noting the n is materially larger and deliberately un-gated would prevent a casual reader from conflating the ~2% coefficient match with a same-sample replication.
2. **State the robustness range source.** The "robust across plausible sample reconstructions (0.080–0.090)" claim recurs in three artifacts but no reconstruction sweep appears in the results JSONs; either cite where those alternative-sample estimates live or soften to "expected to be robust."
3. **CATE framing.** The CATE spline (+0.013…+0.038) is monotone increasing in NonRep and qualitatively supports the paper's mechanism more cleanly than the GATE terciles; consider leading the heterogeneity discussion with the CATE (with its joint band, which dips below zero at low NonRep) rather than the noisier tercile GATE.

### Comments
What works well: the replication gate is implemented and documented exactly as intended — the paper is in hand (open access, PDF shipped), the published Table 5 triple interaction reproduces within reasonable deviation (origination 0.0816 vs 0.080, application 0.0803 vs 0.082, and the near-exact NonRep × post anchor −1.9604 vs −1.966), and the gate PASSes as PARTIAL with a specific, named explanation on every coefficient. The estimand statement matches the report's claims; the continuous-intensity treatment is correctly routed to DID_PLR rather than DoubleMLDIDMulti (router rule 2.1-8b), and the rationale is stated. Control discipline is exemplary and proportionate: the 23 aggregated loan-characteristic shares are kept for replication fidelity but explicitly dropped from the causal conditioning set as post-treatment bad controls, conditioning the extension only on the five exogenous county covariates. Port-vs-run is declared ("port") and the cluster-by-FIPS SE convention is matched end to end. Every number in the report is traceable: the four DML learner coefs (Lasso +0.0207, Forest +0.0203, Boosting +0.0217, Trees +0.0138) match dml_results.json; the gap table's "7 consistent" is honest and correctly tags the loan-level Table 3 (+0.035) benchmark as a "different scale" reference rather than a chased target; sample sizes (8174 triple-diff, 8194 double-diff anchor) are consistent across text, tables, JSONs, and the forest plot. Causal language is proportionate to a DiD design with stated parallel-trends/no-anticipation assumptions and is not upgraded. The single essential item is a one-sentence wording correction on the heterogeneity block, not a problem with the estimates themselves.
