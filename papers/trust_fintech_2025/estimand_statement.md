- **Design:** continuous-intensity difference-in-differences (panel). Treatment = county 2015 Wells-Fargo deposit-share exposure x post-2016-scandal; outcome = FinTech share of mortgage originations/applications.
- **Replication target:** Table 5 county-level TRIPLE difference. The coefficient of interest is the triple interaction `exposure x post x NonRep` (published +0.080 origination / +0.082 application); reproduced here within reasonable deviation (~2%; sign + 5% significance preserved). The package ships no code, so the exact estimation sample (n=7335) is not reproducible; our n is larger and the coefficient is robust across reconstructions.
- **Extension model (router rule 2.1-8b):** `DID_PLR` -- PLR on the two-way (county+year) FE-residualized panel (the DML analogue of a TWFE DiD). `DoubleMLDIDMulti` is NOT applicable: the treatment is a continuous intensity, not binary staggered timing. The extension robustifies the average-county double difference (`exposure x post`) with ML-partialled EXOGENOUS county covariates (the loan-characteristic shares are aggregated post-treatment outcomes and are excluded as potential bad controls).
- **Estimand:** the FE-adjusted effect of exposure-intensity x post on FinTech share, with county and year effects absorbed and covariates partialled out by ML; SE clustered by county. **Because the treatment is a CONTINUOUS intensity, the PLR coefficient is a conditional-variance(of the treatment)-weighted average derivative, not an equally-weighted ATE** (the weights upweight counties with more exposure-variation conditional on the controls + FE).
- **Method & inference notes:** the DML extension runs a SINGLE cross-fitting split (n_rep=1, n_folds=5), so the B&N median-SE adjustment is inactive — inference is DoubleML's analytic SE clustered on 842 county clusters. Results are stable across all four learners (0.014-0.022) and sit on the parametric TWFE anchor (0.0193), so single-split noise is not material. First-stage nuisance fit (out-of-fold, Forest): ml_l (outcome) RMSE ~= 1.32, ml_m (treatment) RMSE ~= 4.82; the treatment is much harder to predict than the outcome (high ml_m RMSE), which is reassuring for identification. The penalized learner uses CV-Lasso on the 5 exogenous controls (valid DML inference, not the BCH post-selection guarantee).
- **Identifying assumptions (inherited):** parallel trends conditional on the controls; no anticipation of the Sept-2016 shock; exposure is as-good-as-assigned conditional on covariates + FE.
- **Data caveat:** the county-level HMDA panel is real; the loan-level headline (Table 3, +0.035) is not reproducible because the 4.3 GB loan-level file is too large to include; the trust-channel Gallup results are pseudo data and out of scope.
