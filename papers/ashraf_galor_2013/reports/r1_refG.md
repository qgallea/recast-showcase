## General Referee Report
**Round:** 1
**Overall verdict:** Accept

### Contribution assessment
This RECAST is an *IV-path test case* deliberately chosen to stress the boundary between a nonlinear (quadratic, hump-shaped) structural model and a linear PLIV DML extension. It contributes (i) an essentially perfect replication of the headline quadratic 2SLS (Ashraf & Galor 2013, Table 2 col. 5), and (ii) an honest *negative result*: the IV→PLIV router yields a single-treatment linear PLIV that cannot represent the hump, and the pipeline labels its own output as functional-form-limited rather than emitting a misleading "diversity effect." Would I be pleased to have produced this? Yes. The value is precisely in surfacing and correctly flagging a known limitation; the replication fidelity is exemplary and the extension's framing is disciplined and proportionate.

### Report consistency
No internal contradictions found. Prose, tables (Tables 1–4), and JSONs agree on all coefficients, SEs, p-values, CIs, n, tiers, and the turning point. The router-nominates-two-instruments / single-treatment-uses-`mdist_addis` distinction (route lists `[mdist_addis, adivhat_sqr]`; the linearized single-treatment PLIV uses `adiv` on `mdist_addis`) is handled coherently and is consistent with the "linearization collapses the design" narrative.

### Essential issues (must be fixed before the result can be trusted)
None — the replication is faithful and the claims are proportionate. Coefficients reproduce to ~10⁻⁴, the turning point matches 0.690 exactly, the PARTIAL is explained as a single last-digit robust-SE rounding artifact, the LINEARIZED PLIV is consistently framed as NOT comparable to the hump (not over-claimed as a diversity effect), n=21 is caveated throughout, and the functional-form limitation is disclosed as the report's central theme.

### Suggestions (non-binding)
1. **State the linearized-slope sign-ambiguity claim more carefully.** §5.1 argues the single linear slope is "near-zero / sign-ambiguous" because the data straddle the peak, yet both learners return *positive* slopes (+7.39, +9.18), and the Lasso slope is nominally significant (p=0.037). The two statements are not contradictory (a positive average projection is consistent with more mass on the left arm), but the report would read more cleanly if it noted that the realized linearized slopes happen to be positive and that this positivity is itself an artifact of sample composition rather than evidence against the hump.
2. **Make the two-instrument vs one-instrument transition explicit in the DML table caption.** Table 4 says "instrument `mdist_addis`" while §5.1 says the router nominated both `mdist_addis` and `adivhat_sqr`. A one-line note in the caption that the linearization to a single treatment necessarily drops the second (generated) instrument would preempt a reader's confusion.

### Comments
What works well: this is a model of disciplined negative reporting. The replication is, in substance, exact (coefficients to ~10⁻⁴; turning point exact at 0.690), and the report resists the temptation to call it SUCCESS — it transparently explains that a single 0.001 last-digit robust-SE difference (88.063 vs 88.064), arising from reconstructing the generated instrument, drives the worst-of PARTIAL tier. The extension is the genuinely informative output: rather than dressing a linear PLIV slope up as "the diversity effect," the pipeline detects the functional-form mismatch via the router's verbatim warning and labels every PLIV number "exploratory," "functional-form-limited," and "NOT comparable." Combined with the prominent n=21 caveat, the claims are well within the evidence. The Phase-2.5 findings (generated-instrument handling needs bespoke support; PLIV support newly added) are appropriately flagged as engineering boundaries, not buried. The two suggestions above are cosmetic and non-binding. I recommend Accept.
