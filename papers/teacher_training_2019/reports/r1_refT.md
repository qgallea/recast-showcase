# DML-Technical Referee Report
**Round:** 1
**Overall verdict:** Major revision

### Benchmark alignment
The implementation is close to the CDDF/B&N canon on most dimensions: the generic-ML proxies are the canonical aux-split construction (B(Z) and S(Z) = m1 − m0 fit on the auxiliary half, evaluated on the main half; `recast_dml.py:662-667`), splits are drawn at the school (cluster) level (`recast_dml.py:649-655`), the known RCT propensity is used (no estimated propensity enters generic ML), BLP/GATES are the weighted CDDF regressions with cluster-robust SEs (`recast_dml.py:675-702`), aggregation is variational (medians, doubled p-values), the Λ-criteria best learner matches the benchmark's neural network, and the IRM side carries `cluster_cols="schid"` through DoubleML (`recast_dml.py:539-544`). Two material deviations remain: (i) the `blockZ` randomization strata — pre-registered in the spec as nuisance fixed effects — never entered any covariate set (proxies, BLP/GATES controls, or IRM nuisances), a design-fidelity break on the headline benchmarked quantity β₂ (BLOCKING); and (ii) the variational CIs are medians of per-split 90% bounds reported as "90% CIs," whereas the CDDF guarantee makes those 1−2α = 80% intervals (the benchmark's convention is per-split 95% bounds → 90% adjusted CIs).

### Essential issues (BLOCKING marked)

1. **[C6/C19] blockZ randomization strata omitted from every estimation covariate set, contradicting the spec** — **BLOCKING (B1)**
   - **Canonical behavior:** The inherited conditioning set must be carried variable-for-variable into the extension (estimand statement, spec §2.3); `paper_spec.json` line 49 records `"fixed_effects": ["blockZ"]` and the `_blocks_note` (line 59) explicitly pre-registers the treatment: blockZ "is a valid stratification control (entered as fixed effects / one-hot in nuisances)." CDDF 2018 applications in stratified RCTs likewise condition on strata.
   - **Observed:** Notebook cell 23 passes only the 24 `controls` to `rd.run_cddf(...)`; cell 7 passes only `controls` to `rd.doubleml_model(...)`. blockZ enters neither the proxy learners, nor the BLP/GATES control set X1 = [1, B] (`recast_dml.py:680-681`), nor the IRM nuisances. The paper itself flags this as candidate explanation (c) (paper.tex §5.5, lines 489-495) — i.e., the deviation was discovered, not chosen.
   - **Why it matters:** β₂ is the headline *benchmarked* quantity and the paper's central finding is its divergence (0.178 vs 0.651, verdict "divergent"). County strata carry systematic teacher/implementation variation — exactly the variation CLAN shows dominating the sorting (teacher college degree, rank, experience, age). Proxies denied that variation produce a noisier S(Z), which attenuates β₂ mechanically (the paper's own candidate (d)). A >20% movement of β₂ = 0.178 from restoring a pre-registered part of the conditioning set is plausible; across all implementation differences combined, β₂ moved from 0.65 to 0.18. The estimand statement's claim "variable-for-variable from the original code" is currently inaccurate, since the original specification includes the strata fixed effects.
   - **Required fix:** In notebook 04, append one-hot blockZ dummies (6 strata) to the covariate list passed to both `rd.run_cddf` (cell 23) and `rd.doubleml_model` (cell 7), and include them in the BLP/GATES X1 controls; re-run the stage-4→6 cascade and regenerate the gap table. This fix is blinding-safe: it is justified by the spec's `_blocks_note`, written at stage 1, before the gap table existed — it is design fidelity, not tuning toward the benchmark. Keep the school-level splits (those are the design-faithful choice; candidate (b) needs no fix).

2. **[C3] n_rep = 20 on the IRM side — disclosed deviation; disclosure acceptable** — ESSENTIAL (E1)
   - **Canonical behavior:** B&N (`ite <- 100`); skill C3 floor of 100, with 20–99 graded ESSENTIAL.
   - **Observed:** `dml_results.n_rep: 20`. The deviation was pre-registered in `config.yaml` (lines 20-26) *before* estimation with a written justification (large n → small cross-fitting MC noise; no benchmark exists for the IRM learner rows; per-cell compute bound), and the paper carries it verbatim with the observed MC dispersions (paper.tex §6, "Disclosed deviation"). Critically, the *benchmarked* quantity kept the full protocol (`n_rep_hte = 100` CDDF splits).
   - **Why it matters / judgment:** The disclosure is acceptable: the affected rows are unbenchmarked, both confirm a null, and the B&N-adjusted SE charges for cross-split variation. One honesty nuance is required: the claim "Monte Carlo cross-fitting noise is small" holds for Forest (mc_sd/SE = 0.019/0.043 ≈ 0.45) but is strained for Boosting (0.054/0.078 ≈ 0.69).
   - **Required fix:** Add one sentence qualifying the Boosting MC dispersion. Since B1 forces a stage-4 re-run anyway, lift n_rep to 100 if the per-cell budget allows; if not, the disclosed n_rep = 20 justification stands as written. No re-run is demanded for this item alone.

3. **[C9 + R2b] Propensity diagnostics: trimmed share unreported; ml_m log-loss mislabeled as RMSE; worse-than-constant propensity fit undisclosed** — ESSENTIAL (E2)
   - **Canonical behavior:** C9 requires trimming to (0.01, 0.99) *with the trimmed share reported*; R2b requires nuisance losses interpreted honestly.
   - **Observed:** `recast_dml.py:551-553` constructs `DoubleMLIRM` with package defaults (truncation at threshold 0.01 in doubleml 0.11), so trimming is active, but no trimmed share appears in `dml_results.json` or the paper. `recast_dml.py:578-579` stores `obj.nuisance_loss` under the key `nuisance_rmse`; for the classifier `ml_m` this is log-loss, yet paper Table 5 notes call all three "Nuisance RMSEs." Both ml_m values (Forest 0.779, Boosting 0.884) exceed the constant-propensity entropy of 0.693 — the estimated propensity is worse than the known constant p = 0.513, which is expected under school-level randomization but is never said.
   - **Why it matters:** The IRM score divides by m̂(1−m̂); a worse-than-chance, overfit propensity is exactly the case where trimming bites and the reader needs the trimmed share (or the range of m̂) to trust the ATE rows. The mislabel makes the propensity column uninterpretable (0.88 "RMSE" on a binary variable would be absurd; as log-loss it is merely poor).
   - **Required fix:** In the B1 re-run, log the trimmed share (or min/max of m̂) per learner into `dml_results.json`; relabel the ml_m entry as log-loss in the table notes; add one sentence noting the propensity learners underperform the known constant (and that the known-propensity option exists in an RCT). Prose + logging only; conclusions (both nulls) are insensitive.

4. **[C1/C14] `interactions_used: true` / "300 engineered features" contradicts the executed code** — ESSENTIAL (E3)
   - **Canonical behavior:** C1 requires the interaction basis only for *penalized* learners; reporting must describe what actually ran.
   - **Observed:** `recast_dml.py:489-493` — `expand_interactions = learner_name == "Lasso"`, so Forest and Boosting were fit on the raw 24 controls. Lasso never ran. Yet notebook cell 22 hard-codes `"interactions_used": True` and `"n_features_after_interactions": 300` into `dml_results.json`, and paper.tex §6 states the IRM was estimated "on the 24 inherited controls plus interactions (300 engineered features)."
   - **Why it matters:** The methodology description of the headline DML extension is factually wrong about its feature set. No estimate changes (tree/boosting learners need no interaction basis — C1's exception logic), but a referee or replicator following the paper would build the wrong design matrix.
   - **Required fix:** Correct the JSON metadata (condition `interactions_used` on a penalized learner actually running) and the §6 sentence to "on the 24 inherited controls (the interaction basis is reserved for penalized learners, none of which ran)."

5. **[C16/R2a] Variational CI convention: medians of per-split 90% bounds reported as 90% CIs** — ESSENTIAL (E4)
   - **Canonical behavior:** CDDF 2018 (NBER WP 24678), variational inference: the median of per-split (1−α) CI bounds has guaranteed coverage only 1−2α; the adjusted p-value is 2·median(p). To report a 90% adjusted CI one medians per-split 95% bounds — which is the convention behind the benchmark's "90% CIs" (`benchmark_results.json` `_method`: "medians over 100 splits, 90% CIs, doubled median p-values").
   - **Observed:** Notebook cell 23 calls `run_cddf(..., alpha=0.10, ...)`; `cddf_one_split` computes `conf_int(alpha=alpha)` → per-split 90% bounds (`recast_dml.py:682-683, 705`), and `run_cddf` medians those bounds (`recast_dml.py:764-785`), reported everywhere as "90% CI." The p-value doubling is correct; the CIs are not — they are 80%-coverage intervals labeled 90%.
   - **Why it matters:** All BLP/GATES intervals in Tables 2-3 and the gap table are too narrow relative to the labeled coverage, and the headline interval comparison (ours [-0.287, 0.639] vs benchmark [0.312, 0.99]) mixes conventions. Correcting *widens* our CIs, so the non-rejection of homogeneity is unchanged — hence essential, not blocking — but the cross-implementation comparison must be like-for-like.
   - **Required fix:** In the B1 re-run, pass per-split alpha = 0.05 and label the medianed bounds as the 90% adjusted CIs (or, at minimum, relabel current intervals as 80%-coverage and say so in the gap-table note).

### Suggestions (non-binding)
1. **[C12] Proxy NN capacity sensitivity.** The CDDF NeuralNet proxy uses (20,) hidden units at aux-half N ≈ 4,800; the capacity rule suggests (100,) at N ≥ 500. A wider-proxy sensitivity is justified by the capacity rule (not by the benchmark gap) and speaks directly to attenuation candidate (d).
2. **[C19] CLAN clustering caveat.** CLAN uses unclustered Welch t-tests (`recast_dml.py:734-739`); for teacher-level variables (constant within school) the effective sample is ~schools, not students, so "every CLAN difference is statistically significant" (paper §5.4) overstates precision. CDDF/B&N practice is also unclustered, so a one-sentence caveat suffices; direction verdicts are unaffected.
3. **Propensity refinements.** p is the student-level treated share on the full sample (0.5130) while estimation uses the complete-case sample (n = 9,676); the design propensity is school-level (101/201 ≈ 0.5025) and stratified by county. In the re-run, compute p on the estimation sample and consider stratum-specific known propensities (and stratum-aware school splits), per CDDF practice in stratified RCTs.
4. **Estimand-statement wording.** "the original paper's selection-on-observables claim" mischaracterizes an RCT — conditional exogeneity holds by randomization; say "guaranteed by randomization (stratified by county)" instead.
5. **[R2e] Render the two-layer label.** Gap-table rows carry only the materiality verdict; persist per-split β₂ draws (analogous to `dml_reps.parquet`) so the MC-implementation layer of `stochastic_agreement` is recomputable for the generic-ML rows.
